Europian budget deficits

Minor programming endproject January 2019; Eefje Roelfsema, 10993673

Most of the countries in the Europian Union have the euro as their currency. Having this many countries using the same currency brings risks; a dicision of 1 country may instabilize the euro, which is a problem for the whole of Europe. Therefore the 28 members of the Europian Union signed the Stability and Growth pact, which is an agreement to acilitate and maintain the stability of the Economic and Monetary Union (EMU). In this agreement there are strict rules on how much a country is allowed to spend. One of the main rules of this agreement, and also the most debated rule, is that EU-countries are not allowed to spend more of 3% of their Gross Domestic Product (GDP) each year. That means that if your budget deficit is smaller than -3%, you're following this rule of the Stability and Growth pact.

Due to the financial crisis that hit Europe in the end of 2008, it became too difficult for most countries to maintain the rules of the Stability and Growth pact. Countries spended too much to get their economy out of this recession. Countries who usually did not violate the pact, came under special supervision of the EU. Since then there is a lot of uncertainty about the budget deficits of Europian Union countries. On this website I try to create clarity on this topic.

Map of the Europian Union

On my homepage their is a Europian Union map, that shows the budget deficit of each country via the colour. Via the slider below this map, you can change the year you are interested in. Next to the worldmap there is a legend, which explains which colour corresponds to which budget deficit percentage. In short:

- Green colour : a country has a positive budget deficit, they're following the rules of the Europian Union. A positive budget deficit also means that they're spending less than they're receving. This is also positive.

- Yellow colour : a country has a negative budget deficit, but it is between 0% and -3%. They are following the rules of the Europian Union, nevertheless they are still spending more than they are receiving.

- Red colour : a country has a negative budget deficit and it is lower than -3%. This means that a country is not following the rules of the Europian Union. The more red a country is the bigger the higher their budget deficit, which is a bad thing.

Government Spendings

A budget deficit is caused by spending too much money. Therefore I made it visible for each country in each year what they're spending. You can also see to which sectors the governemnt is spending the money to.
